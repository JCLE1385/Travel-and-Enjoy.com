Panumbagan Sandbar

Enjoy your holidays

The gorgeous Panumbagan Sandbar stands off the shoreline of Barangay Bantayan Pilar, Sorsogon. For a sandbar, Panumbagan is big and long and can pass off as an islet. It stretches to more than 5-kilometers and features white pebbly sand. Aside from having ocean views, the sandbar also offers panoramas of the Mayon and Bulusan Volcanoes.

Sorsoganons Livelihood

Be amaze to the livelihood of Sorsoganons

Sorsogon, located in the southeastern part of Luzon in the Philippines, is a region rich in cultural and agricultural heritage. It is part of the Bicol Peninsula, which is home to the Bicol people, one of the largest cultural-linguistic groups in the Philippines. The Bicol region, often referred to as "Bicolandia," includes Sorsogon and neighboring islands. The Bicol people are predominantly Roman Catholic and have a culture deeply rooted in agriculture, with rice, coconuts, pili nuts, and Abaca being their primary products. Their language belongs to the Austronesian family, closely related to other languages in the central Philippine.
